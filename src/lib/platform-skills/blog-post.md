# Blog post — writing strategy

## Shape

A blog post is the longer-form sibling of the newsletter issue. Target ~700 words for sharp pieces, up to ~1500 for the deeper arcs. Body reads as editorial — wider column, serif-leaning, bigger headings.

## Opening

Blog posts tolerate slower openings than LinkedIn or newsletter issues. The reader who clicked your blog has already chosen to spend 5 minutes. Don't waste that, but don't burn the lede with a twist that needs a 200-word setup either.

Three patterns:

1. **Scene + question** — open in a specific moment, then ask the question the post answers.
2. **Counter-claim** — "The conventional wisdom about X is wrong." Earned only if the post actually shows it.
3. **Personal context** — "I've spent the last six months thinking about X. Here's where I've landed." Works when the writer's authority is established or being earned in the post itself.

## Section discipline

Each section ~200-400 words. H2 per section. Subsections (H3) only when the argument genuinely splits.

The blog body has more room for examples + parentheticals + quoted passages than the newsletter. Use the room — but every paragraph should still serve the through-line.

## Voice notes

- Slightly more formal than newsletter, slightly less than long-form essay.
- Specifics still beat abstractions.
- Active voice unless the actor genuinely doesn't matter.
- Embedded quotes from the user's own archive land well — drop a line from a past piece, link to it.

## What to avoid

Same banned-word list as newsletter (em-dash pivots, antithesis flips, "crucial," "vital," "delve," "tapestry," "underscore," "showcase"). Plus:

- Long preambles before the actual point.
- Bullet lists of three abstract qualities. Bullets are for genuinely-list-shaped content.
- Closing with a "What about you?" question that the post didn't earn.

## Closer

A blog post can take a longer closer than a LinkedIn post or a newsletter — sometimes a whole paragraph. But the final sentence should still land the post's argument in a way the reader will remember.

## Length notes

700 words for sharp argumentative pieces. 1200-1500 for deeper essays where the reader signed up for a longer ride. Above 2000 and you should ask whether this is a blog post or two.
